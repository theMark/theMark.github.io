magined

Founder & Builder magined AI + commerce

An AI interior designer for everyday people. Upload a photo of any space, pick a style, and see it redesigned in photorealistic detail with real furniture you can shop. Founded and built solo with Claude Code and an AI-assisted dev stack.

context

Inspiration is easy. Acting on it isn't. Anyone redecorating or renovating a space can scroll Pinterest, Instagram, and design blogs for hours and still end up no closer to actually doing it. The gap is between seeing a room you love and knowing where to buy the pieces that make it work.

magined closes that gap for everyday people — not designers, not pros — who just want their space to feel like the one in their head. Upload a photo of any room (or any space at all), pick from twelve styles, and see a photorealistic redesign populated with shoppable furniture. Inspiration on the left, checkout on the right.

my role

Founder and sole builder. Product design, engineering, content, and SEO — shipping the product, the marketing surface, and the AI-assisted dev workflow that lets one person move at this pace.

what I did

A few of the threads:

  • Designed and built the core interior-designer flow — upload a space, pick from twelve styles, see a photorealistic redesign with real furniture matched into it.
  • Pivoted the product based on real user behavior — started as a curated AI-generated gallery people could shop from, then shifted to an AI designer for your own space, with the gallery alongside as inspiration.
  • Built the AI-assisted dev stack — Claude Code as the build environment, Figma MCP and paper.design MCP for mocks and prototypes, and gstack (Garry Tan's Claude Code stack) to refine ideas quickly.
  • Built an LLM/SEO content engine — programmatic style-comparison and room-idea pages, with the Google Search Console MCP wired in for weekly reports on what's ranking and where to push next.

outcome

Live, iterating, and live-learning. Indexed and earning search traffic, growing the catalog of shoppable rooms, and refining the AI designer based on what real users do — not what I assumed they'd do. Still a work in progress, which is the honest state of any first-year founder-built product.
