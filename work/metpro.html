A coach in your pocket, five years in the making

MetPro pairs clients with real nutrition coaches. For five years I was the designer and a developer on the platform — shaping the client and coach experiences in Figma and shipping them in production code on web and mobile.

01 · Context

MetPro started as a high-touch personal coaching service for nutrition and fitness. The product challenge was preserving the quality of a one-on-one coaching relationship while letting the business serve far more clients than coaches could see individually. The software had to feel like a coach in your pocket — not another calorie tracker.

02 · Both sides of the relationship

  • For clients — the mobile experience: daily check-ins, meal logging, progress, and messaging with a coach who knows your name.
  • For coaches — the tools that let one coach carry a much larger roster without the relationship going cold: dashboards that surface who needs attention today, and messaging that keeps the personal touch.
  • For the methodology — working directly with the coaching team to turn their approach into product surfaces that nudged behavior without nagging.

03 · Designer who ships, the early years

This role is where my design-and-build working style was forged — long before AI tooling made it fashionable. I shipped front-end across the web app and the React Native clients on a Ruby on Rails backend, wearing the marketing hat too. When the person who designs the screen also ships it, the gap between intent and product goes to zero.

Five years on one product teaches you what launch week never does: which decisions compound, and which ones you pay for monthly.
