The tools that keep a solar company moving

Behind every install is a chain of office work: scheduling, commissions, permits, pipeline. I designed the internal platform operators live in all day at Freedom Forever — including Lightspeed, the solar project pipeline system, and an AI customer-support agent built on an agent framework.

Role
Product designer
Timeline
2021 — 2026
Platform
Data-dense desktop web
Tools
Figma, Claude Agent SDK, ElevenLabs, Mixpanel

01 Context

A fast-growing national installer runs on back-office throughput: pricing, permits, commissions, scheduling, supplier coordination. That work was spread across spreadsheets, legacy tools, and a growing internal platform — and operators were context-switching between five systems to finish one task.

02 What I worked on

  • Lightspeed — the solar project pipeline management system, where a project's whole life from sale to activation is tracked and moved.
  • Scheduling — matching crews, trucks, and jobs across regions, where a bad screen means a wasted truck roll.
  • Commissions — the tooling that calculates and explains pay for the sales organization, where trust depends on legibility.
  • AI customer support — a support agent and the agent framework behind it, built with the Claude Agent SDK, with ElevenLabs for voice. This is where my design work and AI-builder work merged.

03 Designing for the ten-thousandth use

Operators spend eight hours a day in these tools. That changes what good design means: density becomes kindness, consistency becomes speed, and every extra click is multiplied by hundreds of people, every day. I designed from first principles about the work itself — watching how operators actually moved through a task, then making the software match.

Consumer software gets judged in the first five minutes. Operator software gets judged in the second month.

04 What carried forward

This is where I learned to treat AI agents as a design material. A support agent isn't a chatbot bolted onto a help page — it's a coworker that needs the same things operators do: context, guardrails, and a clear picture of the pipeline. Designing the framework around the agent turned out to matter more than designing its chat window.
