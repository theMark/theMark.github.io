Two mobile apps for a national solar operation

At Freedom Forever, a residential solar installer operating across the United States, I designed the mobile apps on both sides of an install: the field app the crews work from, and the customer app homeowners use to follow their project.

Role
Product designer
Timeline
2021 — 2026
Platform
iOS & Android, mobile-first
Tools
Figma, Claude Code, Mixpanel

01 · Context

A residential solar install moves through dozens of milestones over weeks — site survey, permitting, install, inspection, activation — and it involves field crews, project managers, and an anxious homeowner who just signed a 25-year commitment. The tools holding that together were built for the office; the work happens on driveways and rooftops.

02 · The field app

The field app is the crew's window into the job: schedules, site details, documents, and status updates from a phone, often with gloves on and sun on the screen. I designed it for one-handed use and fast scanning — the measure of success was how little time a tech had to spend in it.

Instrumentation mattered as much as the interface: we used Mixpanel to see where crews actually got stuck, and let that reshape the flows.

03 · The customer app

The customer app gives homeowners a clear view of a long, opaque process — where their project stands, what happens next, and who to talk to. Fewer surprises for the homeowner means fewer support calls for the operation.

This one I both designed and coded, working in Figma and Claude Code side by side. Designing in the production medium collapsed the usual handoff cycle: what I mocked in the morning could be running on a device by the afternoon.

The fastest design review is a build on your phone.

04 · What carried forward

Designing both sides of the same operation taught me that the field app and the customer app are really one product — every status a crew logs is a moment of reassurance (or anxiety) for a homeowner. Designing the pair together, on a shared component language, is what made each one simpler.
