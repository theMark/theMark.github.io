freedom forever — field app

Product Designer Freedom Forever Mobile-first field tooling

A mobile-first field app for installers, surveyors, inspectors, and permit runners at Freedom Forever. Replaced a clunky legacy portal with one-tap access to schedules, projects, and the information field crews actually need on a worksite.

context

Before the new app, field employees — installers, surveyors, inspectors, permit runners — had to sign into an outdated portal to find the information they needed for the day's jobs. The sign-in flow itself was painful (a separate identity system that didn't play nicely with their other tools), and once they were in, getting to the right project took more taps than a worksite has time for.

Field work doesn't tolerate friction. Field employees are rarely sitting at a desk — they're on a roof, in a driveway, often in direct sunlight, sometimes with a glove on, almost always doing five things at once. The replacement app had to assume those conditions, not fight them.

my role

Product designer on the field app. Partnered with field employees across roles — installers, surveyors, inspectors, permit runners — to understand how a day actually flowed. Owned the visual and interaction design, and built the design system that scaled across the app. Engineers shipped the code.

what I did

A few of the threads:

  • Interviewed and shadowed field employees across roles to map the real flow of a job — what they check first thing, what they need by 8 AM, what slows them down at 3 PM.
  • Designed for on-site use — large tap targets, high-contrast surfaces readable in direct sunlight, and flows that work with a glove on or one hand free.
  • Built a design system tuned to the field app's specific needs — patterns engineers could compose into new screens without re-designing common interactions every time.
  • Made schedule access the front door of the app. The single most important thing a field employee needs to see when they open the app is what they're doing today and where.

outcome

Strong qualitative reception from the people doing the work. Field employees consistently called out two things: how much easier the app was to use, and how fast it felt compared to the legacy portal. The app became the daily home base for installers, surveyors, inspectors, and permit runners across a national install footprint.
